ANGER   الغضب

الغضب مفتاح كل شر

Anger is the Key to All Evils

1ـ رسول الله‏ (ص): الغضب جمرة من الشيطان.

1– The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘Anger is a smouldering ember [kindled by] Satan.’[Bihar al-Anwar, v. 73, p. 265, no. 15]

2ـ الإمام علي (ع): الحدة ضرب من الجنون لأن صاحبها يندم، فإن لم يندم فجنونه مستحكم.

2– Imam Ali (AS) said, ‘Rage is a type of madness because the one enraged feels regret later on, and if he does not feel regret, then his madness has become ingrained.’[Nahj al-Balagha, Saying 255]

3ـ الإمام الصادق (ع): الغضب مفتاح كل شر.

3– Imam al-Sadiq (AS) said, ‘Anger is the key to all evils.’[al-Kafi, v. 2, p. 303, no. 3]

4ـ الإمام الصادق (ع): الغضب ممحقة لقلب الحكيم.

4– Imam al-Sadiq (AS) said, ‘Anger is such that it destroys the heart of even the wise man.’[al-Kafi, v. 2, p. 305, no. 13]

الحث على ملك الغضب

Enjoinment of Controlling One’s Anger

5ـ رسول الله‏ (ص): ألا اخبركم بأشدكم ؟ من ملك نفسه عند الغضب .

5– The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘Shall I tell you who is the toughest and strongest from among you? The one who controls himself when he is angry.’[Nathr al-Durar, v. 1, p. 183]

6ـ الإمام الباقر (ع): لا قوة كرد الغضب .

6– Imam al-Baqir (AS) said, ‘There is no strength like being able to repel one’s anger.’[Tuhaf al-`Uqoul, no. 286]

7ـ الإمام الصادق (ع): من لم يملك غضبه لم يملك عقله .

7– Imam al-Sadiq (AS) said, ‘He who has no control over his anger has no control over his reason.’[al-Kafi, v. 2, p. 305, no. 13]

الحث على كظم الغيظ

Enjoinment of Suppressing One’s Anger

8ـ رسول الله‏ (ص): إن لجهنم بابا لا يدخلها إلا من شفى غيظه بمعصية الله‏ تعالى.

8– The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘Verily there is a door to Hell, the entrance into which will be reserved for one who vented his anger through committing an act of disobedience to Allah, most High.’[Tanbih al-Khawatir, v. 1, p. 121]

9ـ رسول الله (ص): من كف غضبه كف الله‏ عنه عذابه.

9– The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘He who curbs his anger, Allah will curb His punishment from him.’[Bihar al-Anwar, v. 73, p. 263, no. 7]

10ـ الإمام الباقر (ع): من كظم غيظا وهو يقدر على إمضائه حشا الله‏ قلبه أمنا وإيمانا يوم القيامة.

10– Imam al-Baqir (AS) said, ‘He who suppresses his anger despite being able to vent it, Allah will fill his heart with peace and security on the Day of Resurrection.’[al-Kafi, v. 2, p. 110, no. 7]

11ـ الإمام الصادق (ع): نعم الجرعة الغيظ لمن صبر عليها ... .

11– Imam al-Sadiq (AS) said, ‘What a good dose anger is for he who can swallow it …’[al-Kafi, v. 2, p. 109, no. 3]

دواء الغضب

The Remedy for Anger

12ـ رسول الله‏ (ص): يا علي، لاتغضب، فإذا غضبت فاقعد وتفكر في قدرة الرب على العباد وحلمه عنهم، وإذا قيل لك: اتق الله‏ فانبذ غضبك، وراجع حلمك .

12– The Prophet (SAWA) said, ‘O Ali, do not get angry, and if you do get angry, then sit down and reflect upon the power of your Lord over His creation and His clemency towards them in spite of it. And whenever anyone tells you [condescendingly] to fear Allah, expel your anger and remind yourself of your clemency.’[Tuhaf al-`Uqoul, no. 14]

13ـ الإمام علي (ع): داووا الغضب بالصمت، والشهوة بالعقل .

13– Imam Ali (AS) said, ‘Remedy your anger with silence, and your carnal desire through your reason.’[Ghurar al-Hikam, no. 5155]

مدح الغضب لله

The Praiseworthiness of Anger for the Sake of Allah

14ـ الإمام زين العابدين (ع): قال موسى‏بن عمران (ع): يا رب، من أهلك الذين تظلهم في ظل عرشك يوم لا ظل إلا ظلك ؟ فأوحى الله‏ إليه: ... والذين يغضبون لمحارمي إذا استحلت‏ مثل‏النمر إذاجرح!

14– Prophet Moses (AS) [addressed Allah] saying, ‘O Lord, who are your special people whom You will shade with the shade of Your Throne on the day when no shade will avail except Your Shade?’ So Allah revealed to him, ‘… and those who are angered when the things that I have prohibited are deemed lawful [by people], the way a leopard is angered when it is wounded!’[Wasael al-Shi`aah , v. 11, p. 416, no. 3]

15ـ الإمام علي (ع): كان (ص) لايغضب للدنيا، فإذا أغضبه الحق لم يعرفه أحد ولم يقم لغضبه شيء حتى ينتصر له.

15– Imam Ali (AS) said, ‘He (SAWA) [i.e. the Prophet] never used to get angry over worldly matters, but when he did get angry for the sake of the truth, he was unrecognisable and nothing could restrain his anger until he had triumphed in his case [for the truth].’[al-Mahajjat al-Baydha, v. 5, p. 303]

16ـ الإمام علي (ع): من شنئ الفاسقين وغضب لله‏، غضب الله‏ له وأرضاه يوم القيامة.

16– Imam Ali (AS) said, ‘He who displays his ill-feeling towards the immoral people and gets angry for the sake of Allah, Allah will get angry for his sake and will render him well-pleased on the Day of Resurrection.’[Nahj al-Balagha, Saying 31]
